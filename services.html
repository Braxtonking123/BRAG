Windshield Replacement
Your windshield plays a crucial role in protecting occupants during a collision by absorbing impact and preventing objects from entering the vehicle.
Vent Glass
Vent glass is a small, triangular piece of glass on a car that's located on the side of the vehicle, near the windshield or front door.
Door Glass
A car door glass is the window on the side of a car door. This glass is made from tempered glass and is much stronger than ordinary glass.
Quarter Glass
Quarter glass, also known as a valence window or rear vent glass, is a side window on a vehicle that's usually smaller than the standard passenger windows. is the last window on the side of a vehicle. It is behind the doors and above the rear wheel.
Back Glass
A car back glass, also known as a rear windshield or rear window is the glass pane at the back of a vehicle that faces away from the windshield. Many rear windshields have a defroster made of a metal grid that melts ice and prevents it from freezing.
Sunroof
A sunroof is a panel installed in the roof of a car that can be opened, either by sliding or tilting, to allow light and air into the vehicle's interior; it is typically made of glass and can be manually or electronically controlled depending on the car model.
Removal and Install
When the windshield has damage that compromises the structural integrity or visibility, posing a safety risk to the vehicle occupants; it requires removing the old windshield and installing a new one in its place.
RV/Motorhome
RV windshield replacement is a process that requires special tools and expert training.
Semi
Replacement of the windshield on a semi-truck involves removing the damaged windshield, carefully measuring and fitting a new, custom-sized windshield specifically designed for the truck, and securely bonding it into the frame using specialized tools and adhesives, requiring expertise due to the larger size and unique design of semi truck windshields compared to regular cars; Our trained and licensed auto glass technicians are experienced in heavy-duty vehicles.
Window Regulator
The window regulator is the mechanical assembly that moves a window up and down when power is supplied to an electric motor, or with manual windows, the crank is turned. Window regulators are mounted inside the door panel, out of sight and out of mind until they don't work.
Rock and Chip Repair
Rock chips smaller than one inch in diameter can usually be repaired.
Headlight Restoration
Over time, headlights become foggy or yellow due to exposure to UV rays, which causes the plastic lens to oxidize. Headlight restoration is the process of cleaning and polishing a car's headlights to remove oxidation and discoloration, restoring their clarity and brightness by essentially sanding away the damaged outer layer of the plastic lens and applying a protective sealant to prevent further degradation from UV rays and environmental factors, greatly improving visibility on the road at night by allowing more light to shine through the headlight lens.

Calibration

ADAS Calibration

ADAS calibration is important for maintaining the accuracy and functionality of a vehicle's ADAS systems. This helps to ensure that the systems are performing as designed and maintaining optimal safety standards.

Dynamic Calibration

Performed using a specialized scan tool to calibrate during a road test. This test is to align the camera while driving the vehicle, in the lane over specific time and speed to calibrate to the widest view of the camera.

Static Calibration

Performed using specific targets and a specialized scan tool to align the camera at a specific distance, height and width from the vehicle.

Static/Dynamic Combination

There are many vehicles which will require both static and dynamic calibration performed in sequence.

Adaptive cruise control

Can maintain a chosen velocity and distance between a vehicle and the vehicle ahead.

Anti‐lock braking systems (ABS)

Restores traction to a car’s tires by regulating the brake pressure when the vehicle begins to skid.

Automatic parking

Takes over control of parking functions, including steering, braking, and acceleration.

Backup cameras

Offer driver’s aid when backing up by providing a viewpoint that typically is a blind spot in many traditional cars.

Blind spot monitors

Cameras that monitor the driver’s blind spots and notify the driver of any obstacles that come close to the vehicle (may also work in conjunction with emergency braking systems).

Collision avoidance system

Uses small radar detectors to determine the car’s vicinity to nearby obstacles and notifies the driver of potential collisions. Radar uses radio waves to determine inputs such as range, angle, and velocity of objects.

Cruise control

Can maintain a specific pre‐determined speed, by the driver.

Driver drowsiness detection

Designed to prevent collisions due to driver fatigue. The vehicle obtains information, such as facial patterns, steering movement, driving habits, turn signal use, and diving velocity, to determine if the driver’s activities correspond with drowsy driving. If drowsy driving is suspected, the vehicle typically will sound an alert and/or may vibrate the driver’s seat.

Forward Collision Warning

Monitors the speed of the vehicle and the vehicle in front of it. It sends an alert to the driver of a possible impending collision if your vehicle gets too close to the vehicle in front of it.

Intelligent speed adaptation or intelligent speed advice (ISA)

Assists drivers with compliance to the speed limit. Some ISA systems allow the vehicle to adjust its speed to adhere to the relative speed limit, while other systems only provide a notification to the driver.

Lane departure warning system

Alerts the driver when they partially merge into a lane without using their turn signals in case they unintentionally begin to drift. This system does not take control of the vehicle to help sway the car back into the safety zone but instead sends an audio or visual alert to the driver. (Note: After a windshield installation, an improperly calibrated camera may adversely affect the accuracy of this system.)
